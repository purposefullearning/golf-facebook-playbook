Step 2 — Content Strategy

Content That Resonates With Your Audience

Your 55–65+ audience values nostalgia, course beauty, practical tips, and community — not viral stunts or trick shots.

Audience-First Content Principle

Your audience is experienced golfers who have played for decades. They want: beautiful course content that makes them want to play, gear that solves real problems, nostalgic moments that remind them why they love the game, and community debates about the sport they are passionate about.

01

Florida Course Reviews

Film honest, beautiful reviews of courses in the Tampa Bay area. Florida golf is aspirational for your international audience and relatable for your US audience.

02

Gear Reviews for Mature Golfers

Review clubs and training aids through the lens of a golfer over 55 — slower swing speeds, joint comfort, forgiveness. This is an underserved niche your audience will love.

03

"What Would You Do?" Scenarios

Rules situations, course management dilemmas, etiquette debates. This format drives massive comment volume from experienced golfers who have strong opinions. Post 2–3 per week.

04

Nostalgia & Classic Golf

Throwback moments, classic course architecture, legendary players from the 70s–90s. Your 65+ audience lived through the golden era of golf. Nostalgia content gets shared more than anything else.

05

Family Golf Content

You have a brother in Tampa and a father in Sarasota — that is a built-in content series. "Playing golf with my dad in Sarasota" creates authentic, emotional content that performs extremely well with your demographic.

06

Gear-Powered Giveaways

Once you have gear partnerships, run monthly giveaways. "Comment + subscribe to our email list to enter" converts followers to subscribers at 3–8x normal rates.

Step 5 — Email List Building

Converting Followers Into Email Subscribers

Your Facebook followers are rented — Meta can change the algorithm tomorrow and your reach disappears. An email list is an asset you own forever.

⚠

Why This Is Urgent: Facebook organic reach for pages has dropped from ~16% in 2012 to under 5% today. If you have 5,000 followers and only 250 see your posts, you are one algorithm change away from losing your audience. An email list of 1,000 engaged subscribers is worth more than 10,000 Facebook followers you cannot reliably reach.

Choose a Free Email Platform

Start with MailerLite (free up to 1,000 subscribers, beginner-friendly) or Beehiiv (free up to 2,500, built for content creators). Both allow you to create sign-up landing pages without a website.

Recommended: MailerLite (free) or Beehiiv (free)

Create a Lead Magnet (The "Bribe")

Nobody gives their email for nothing. Create a simple, high-value freebie: "The 10 Best Public Golf Courses in Tampa Bay (Ranked)", "5 Swing Fixes for Golfers Over 55", or "The Florida Golf Course Bucket List." A simple PDF is enough — create it free in Canva.

Format: 1-page PDF created free in Canva

Pin a Sign-Up Post to the Top of Your Page

Post: "I just put together a free guide to the 10 best public golf courses in Tampa Bay — comment 'GUIDE' below and I'll send it to you!" Then DM everyone who comments with your email sign-up link. This is the single highest-converting tactic for Facebook pages.

Automate with ManyChat (free tier)

Use Giveaways to Accelerate Growth

Once you have gear partnerships, run a monthly giveaway: "Win a [Ghost Golf bag / Stix club set] — enter by subscribing to our free newsletter." Giveaways convert followers to email subscribers at 3–8x the normal rate. A single giveaway can add 200–500 subscribers in a week.

Tool: KingSumo (free) or Gleam.io

Send a Weekly Email (Keep It Simple)

One email per week. Format: 1 course tip + 1 gear recommendation + 1 Florida course spotlight + 1 question for the reader. Keep it under 400 words. Build a habit — your subscribers should look forward to your email every week.

Send every Tuesday at 7am EST

Add Your Sign-Up Link Everywhere

Put your email sign-up link in: your Facebook page bio, every video description, every gear review post, and your pinned post. For the next 6 months, every piece of content should have one CTA: "subscribe to the email list."

Goal: 500 subscribers in 90 days
